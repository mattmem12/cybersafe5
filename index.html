A Trojan Horse is a software that appears to be helpful or legitimate. When in reality it is harmful code that damages or steals information. Trojan Horse malware tricks a user into downloading it by imitating a reliable web service that a user would find helpful. A common example of a Trojan Horse is a pop up of an anti malware software that will prevent your computer from being hacked when downloaded but in reality you are downloading a hack. Once a Trojan is downloaded and looks for financial information, encrypts files, and hijack your device to take down other devices.
